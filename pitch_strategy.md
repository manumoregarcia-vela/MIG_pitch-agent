# pitch_strategy

## Narrative angle
Mixed audience strategy: open with undeniable gameplay fantasy, then bridge directly into traction and execution proof. Balance publisher confidence (marketability + launch readiness) with investor confidence (scalable upside + milestone discipline). Current strength center: traction, team.

## Scoring breakdown (1-5)
- **product**: 4/5 — Gameplay clarity and unique hook are well evidenced.
- **traction**: 5/5 — Based on quantity and concreteness of KPIs / external validation signals.
- **market**: 4/5 — Market opportunity framing is explicit.
- **business_model**: 4/5 — Monetization and go-to-market are clear enough for a first pass.
- **team**: 5/5 — Execution credibility inferred from team completeness and leadership coverage.
- **ask**: 5/5 — Ask is specific and decision-friendly.

## Key selling points
- Gameplay-first pitch opening to win attention in under 30 seconds.
- Evidence-led traction narrative: only hard signals, no buzzword inflation.
- Competitive differentiation framed through player fantasy + execution edge.
- Clear milestone ask that maps partner capital to measurable outcomes.
- Team credibility supports delivery risk mitigation.
- Development stage clarity: Vertical slice completed.

## Risks
- Overloading slides with detail despite strong fundamentals.

## How to position them
- Keep deck to high-signal proof points and use backup slides for depth.

## Weakness detection + compensation
- No critical weaknesses detected. Maintain momentum and keep proof density high.

## Recommended slide order (5-8 max)
1. **Hook + Gameplay Clarity** — Show the core loop and player fantasy immediately.
2. **Traction Signals** — Present KPIs, wishlists, tests, and external validation.
3. **Differentiation** — Explain why this game wins versus direct alternatives.
4. **Execution Credibility** — Prove team capability, roadmap realism, and delivery plan.
5. **Business Model + Market** — Show monetization logic and market timing for upside.
6. **The Ask + Next Milestones** — Make the partnership decision clear and time-bound.
